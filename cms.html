History

The Foundation of the Cordis Mariae School roots back to 2007 as a pre-school, Kiddie Care Service [KCS], housed in a single-room quarter adjacent to the consumer service coop store, was treated as one of the services of the Mabuting Pastol Credit Cooperative [MPMC], a primary coop engaged in credit, savings and savings mobilization as its main business with other services, including the pre-school, computer rentals and consumer services.


The diversification in the service offerings, led to the amendment of the Articles of Cooperation and By-Laws of MPMC [1998] and its transformation into a multi-purpose cooperative and re-registration as Mabuting Pastol Multi-purpose Cooperative [MPMC] in 2011.


The evolution of the Kiddie Care Services [KCS] from pre-school to Cordis Mariae School into an elementary School started in 2009. As a requirement for securing a government permit to operate, the law [R.A ] requires a registration certificate from the Security Exchange Commision [SEC]. However, owing to the inclusion of education cooperative in among the types of cooperatives in the Philippines in R.A. 9520 [Amended Cooperative Code of the Philippines 2008] it became imperative that the CMS be registered not at the SEC but at the Cooperative Development Authority [D].


Thus, on October 2, 2010, after a pre-membership education that tacked Rule 2 of the IRR of RA 9520, the Cordis Mariae School was organized as a subsidiary cooperative of MPMC with its own governing Board of Trustees, a distinct organizational structure and Articles of Cooperation and By-Laws. With the evolution of the pre-school, the KCS Team tasked to organize and operate the pre-school similarly evolved as CMS Team, a core group who studied, maintained and recommended to the MPMC Board of Directors development measures. In 2010 CMS was organized, 2011 granted the Registration Certificate from CDA, and in September 2012 secured the Government Permit to Operate Pre-School and Primary School Courses. In March 2013, DepEd grant the CMS the authority to offer a Full Elementary Course.


Cordis Mariae School is used as a tribute to the ICM missionary sisters led by Sister Simmone Coene of the Immaculati Cordis Mariae [ICM] congregation who supported the livelihood and health programs of the Mabuting Pastol Parish where the CMS is situated. Cordis Mariae is a Latin word meaning Heart of Mary.

Requirements for Enrollment

  1. Birth Certificate (NSO)
    • Photocopy (Junior and Senior Kinder)
    • Original (NSO) and photocopy for Grade I and Transferees
  2. Baptismal Certificate
  3. Medical records if available
  4. Certificate of Good Moral Character
  5. Report Card and Form 137 (Transferees)

    6. Entrance Test for new enrollees/transferees P100.00
