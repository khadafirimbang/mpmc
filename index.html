History

Mabuting Pastol Multi-Purpose Cooperative (MPMC) was founded on May 24, 1998, at the Mabuting Pastol Parish, Barangay Commonwealth, Quezon City. Its origins trace back to the "Impukan," a livelihood project initiated by the Livelihood Committee of the parish, guided by Sister Simonne Coene and other CICM Sisters, along with Fr. Guido Everaert, ICM.


The "Impukan" project was inspired by the best practices of scavengers in Barangay Payatas through a learning program at the Ina ng Lupang Pangako Parish. This initiative provided sustainable livelihood opportunities while also learning from the resilience and resourcefulness of marginalized communities.


A group of faithful mothers from the Mabuting Pastol Parish, who regularly gathered for weekly sharing through the Basic Ecclesial Community (BEC), came together with a common vision. They decided to pool their resources, each member contributing what they could from their family’s small income. Their unity and collaboration raised over 25,000 pesos, which became the foundation for the cooperative's establishment.


The cooperative was officially founded by 25 founding cooperators, whose dedication and collective effort led to the formation of MPMC, and shared a common vision: to uplift their families' lives and create opportunities for economic growth based on cooperative principles. Their goal was not just to form a cooperative but also to empower each member and build a strong community where service and solidarity are prioritized. These founding members were: Louie Arenal, Del Asi, Edna Balbarona, Presy Baquiring, Rene Baylon, Josie Baylon, Gloria Benavidez, Diosa Castro, Lilia De Leon, Jeannette Dumo, Regina Furton, Naty Martinez, Mila Obrero, Arceli Ogardo, Nenita Palomar, Teresita Prado, Isabel Perez, Corazon Permelona, Irene Raagas, Julia Rayco, Zosima Riel, Wilfredo Solen Sr., Lina Untalan, and Vic Velasco.


From this humble beginning, the cooperative’s founders worked together to build a foundation for improving their families' lives and creating economic opportunities grounded in cooperative principles.


Rooted in Christian faith, MPMC has continued to empower its members through education and capacity-building programs, equipping them with the knowledge and skills to enhance their personal and economic growth. Beyond being a financial institution, MPMC has remained a platform for social justice and economic progress, enabling members to achieve sustainable livelihoods and financial security.


As an active participant in the global cooperative movement, MPMC also addresses economic, political, and educational needs, fostering shared responsibility and mutual progress. Through ongoing research and developing Filipino values, the cooperative strengthens its structure and collaboration with other cooperatives.


Through the years, Mabuting Pastol Multi-Purpose Cooperative has stayed true to its mission: "Service above all, towards a full and meaningful life." With an unwavering commitment to its members, MPMC continues to be a beacon of hope, empowering individuals and communities through the spirit of cooperativism.

Vision

Pinapangarap ng Mabuting Pastol Multi-Purpose Cooperative (MPMC) ang isang Samahang matatag at may dangal at pinagbuklod ng Pananampalatayang Kristiyano na nagpupunyagi para sa isang malawak, malalim at makahulugang pantao sa pamamagitan ng sama-samang paglalaan ng sarili upang itaguyod ang pangkalahatang kaunlaran tungo sa buhay ng ganap at kasiya-siya

Mission

Bigyang kapangyarihan ang mga kasapi sa pamamagitan ng patuloy na pag aaral at paghuhubog ayon sa diwa at pamantayan ng kilusang kooperatiba

Itaguyod ang gawaing kooperatiba bilang daan tungo sa kaunlarang pangkabuhayan, pampulitika at katarungang panlipunan bilang kaagapay sa mabungang pakikisalamuha sa pandaigdigang pamayanan ng kilusang kooperatiba.

Patuloy na pananaliksik at paglinang sa kaugaliang Pilipino na makakatulong sa pag-ulan ng Mabuting Pastol Multi-purpose Cooperative at iba pang mga Kooperatiba
